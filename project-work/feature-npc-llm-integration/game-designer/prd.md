## Problem Statement

The vast majority of NPCs on our server are silent. Of 67,530 NPC types in the database (61,289 spawned in-world), only about 7,700 have quest scripts that let them respond to player speech. The remaining ~53,500 spawned NPCs — guards, merchants, wandering townsfolk, soldiers, innkeepers — say nothing when a player talks to them. A player who walks up to a Qeynos guard and says "What should I watch out for around here?" gets silence in return.

For a 1–6 player server, this silence is especially noticeable. In a live server with hundreds of players, the world feels alive through other players. On our server, the player and their companions may be the only ones in a zone. When the NPCs around them are mute props, the world feels empty and static.

Phase 1 addresses this by giving every eligible unscripted NPC the ability to hold a basic conversation. When a player speaks to a guard, the guard responds like a guard — talking about local threats, giving directions, reacting to the player's faction standing. When a player greets a merchant, the merchant talks about their wares or local trade. The world becomes a place where inhabitants actually inhabit it.

This is also foundational infrastructure. The LLM sidecar service, Lua bridge module, and global hook established in Phase 1 will be reused by later phases (memory, quest integration, NPC personality) and potentially by other features like the companion recruitment system, where natural NPC dialogue could replace menu-driven recruitment flows.

## Goals

1. **Bring silent NPCs to life** — Every unscripted NPC with sufficient intelligence (INT >= 30) responds to player speech with contextually appropriate, in-character dialogue generated by a local LLM.
2. **Faction shapes the conversation** — NPC responses reflect the player's faction standing across all 9 EQ faction levels, from warm and helpful (Ally) to curt warnings (Threatening) to refusal (Scowling). The same guard gives directions to an ally but tells an apprehensive outsider to move along.
3. **City culture shapes the voice** — A Qeynos guard sounds different from a Freeport guard. A Neriak merchant sounds different from a Kelethin shopkeeper. NPCs reflect the culture, patron deity, and politics of their home city.
4. **No existing content is affected** — NPCs that already have quest scripts continue to work identically. The LLM layer only activates for NPCs that currently say nothing.
5. **The delay feels natural** — A "thinking" emote pattern makes the 1–2 second LLM inference time feel like the NPC is considering their response, not like the server is lagging.
6. **The service is resilient** — If the LLM sidecar is unavailable, NPCs simply fall through to their current silent behavior. No crashes, no error messages, no degraded gameplay.

## Non-Goals

- **No persistent memory** — Phase 1 conversations are stateless. The NPC does not remember previous exchanges. (Phase 2 adds Pinecone-backed memory.)
- **No quest script integration** — NPCs with existing quest scripts are untouched. Adding LLM fallback to scripted NPCs is Phase 3.
- **No NPC-to-NPC conversation** — NPCs only respond to player speech.
- **No custom NPC backstories** — Phase 1 uses only the NPC's database attributes (name, race, class, level, zone, faction) plus zone cultural context to build personality. Rich backstory seeding is Phase 4.
- **No C++ server modifications** — Phase 1 is entirely Lua scripts + Python sidecar + Docker configuration.
- **No async/non-blocking HTTP** — The synchronous `io.popen`/curl approach is acceptable for 1–6 players. Async C++ bindings are Phase 3 if needed.
- **No player language system integration** — NPC responses are always in Common Tongue regardless of what language the player speaks in.
- **No voice or audio integration** — Text-only, delivered through the standard EQ chat window.
- **No admin UI** — Toggling LLM per-NPC is done via data buckets or GM commands, not a web interface.

## User Experience

### Player Flow

1. **The player approaches an NPC** — The player targets any NPC in the world. This could be a gate guard in Freeport, a fisherman on the Qeynos docks, a Vah Shir merchant in Shar Vahl, or a dark elf priestess in Neriak.

2. **The player speaks** — The player types `/say Hello, what can you tell me about this area?` (or any natural speech — questions, greetings, requests for directions, lore inquiries, even insults).

3. **Immediate feedback** — Within a fraction of a second, the NPC performs a "thinking" emote visible to the player: *"Guard Hanlon considers your words carefully..."* This tells the player the NPC heard them and is formulating a response.

4. **The NPC responds** — After 1–2 seconds, the NPC speaks in the chat window with a contextually appropriate response. The response reflects:
   - **Who the NPC is** — A guard talks like a guard. A merchant talks like a merchant. A shaman references spirits and ancestors.
   - **Where they are** — A guard in Qeynos references gnoll threats from Blackburrow and swears by Rodcet Nife. A guard in Freeport references the Militia and Lucan D'Lere's orders. A wood elf ranger in Kelethin watches for Crushbone orcs.
   - **How they feel about the player** — An allied NPC is warm and forthcoming. An indifferent NPC is polite but reserved. A threatening NPC issues a warning. A scowling NPC refuses to engage.

5. **The conversation can continue** — The player can say more things. Each exchange is independent (no memory in Phase 1), but the NPC's responses remain consistent with their identity and the player's faction standing.

6. **Transparent boundaries** — If the player targets an animal, a mindless skeleton, a stone golem, or a fire elemental, speaking produces no NPC response (same as today). If the NPC has a quest script, the script handles speech (same as today). The LLM layer is invisible — it only fills gaps that were previously silent.

### Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Friendly Guard in South Qeynos**

A level 12 half-elf ranger with Ally faction to the Qeynos guards targets Guard Hanlon and types:

> /say Hail, any trouble around here lately?

*Guard Hanlon considers your words carefully...*

> Guard Hanlon says, 'Well met, ranger. The Sabertooth gnolls have grown bolder of late, raiding caravans on the road to Qeynos Hills. If you are heading north, keep your bow at the ready. May the Prime Healer watch over you.'

**Scenario 2: Threatening Guard in Neriak**

The same half-elf ranger (with Threatening faction to the Indigo Brotherhood) targets a Neriak gate guard:

> /say I just want to pass through.

*Darkguard Kaylorn studies you briefly...*

> Darkguard Kaylorn says, 'You have three seconds to turn around, half-breed. State your business or I will state it for you — to the magistrate, over your corpse.'

**Scenario 3: Scowling Guard in Neriak**

The same ranger (now at Scowling faction) tries again:

> /say Hello there.

*Darkguard Kaylorn glares at you with undisguised contempt.*

No verbal response. The NPC refuses all conversation at Scowling. Further speech attempts within 60 seconds are also ignored.

**Scenario 4: Merchant in East Commonlands**

A level 20 human wizard with Indifferent faction targets a traveling merchant:

> /say What do you have for sale?

*Merchant Talia considers your words carefully...*

> Merchant Talia says, 'I carry provisions for travelers passing through the Commonlands — rations, water flasks, and a few torches. If you are heading east toward Befallen, I would stock up. Dark places have a way of wearing through supplies faster than you would expect.'

**Scenario 5: Gruff Freeport Guard**

A level 8 human rogue with Amiable faction to the Freeport Militia targets a gate guard:

> /say Any news?

*Guard Brixton ponders your question...*

> Guard Brixton says, 'News? I have been standing at this gate for twelve hours. The Deathfist orcs are still out there, the tunnel rats are still in the sewers, and Lucan still has not given us a pay raise. That is all the news I have.'

**Scenario 6: Non-Speaking NPC**

The player targets a large rat:

> /say Hello?

Nothing happens. The rat does not respond. (Same behavior as today — rats have INT well below 30 and are non-sentient creatures.)

**Scenario 7: Scripted NPC Unchanged**

The player targets a Priestess in the Temple of Life (who has a quest script):

> /say Hail

> Priestess of Life says, 'Blessings of Rodcet Nife upon you, traveler...' (existing scripted response)

The LLM is never involved. The local quest script handles this NPC's dialogue as it always has.

## Game Design Details

### Mechanics

#### Which NPCs Respond

An NPC responds via the LLM when ALL of these conditions are met:

- **No local quest script exists** for this NPC (the existing quest dispatch chain handles this naturally — local scripts take priority over the global hook)
- **NPC is sentient** (INT >= 30) — The intelligence threshold is a sentience check, not an IQ test. NPCs below INT 30 are non-sentient creatures incapable of speech: animals, mindless undead, insects, basic vermin. This is not a commentary on NPC "intelligence" — an Ogre merchant in Oggok (INT 40) speaks in simple, direct sentences appropriate to Ogre culture, while a High Elf scholar (INT 150) speaks with elaborate formality. Both are sentient and capable of conversation; the INT filter only excludes non-sentient creatures.
- **NPC is not in an excluded creature category** — Certain creature types are categorically non-verbal regardless of INT:
  - **Animals** (body type 21) — wolves, bears, bats, snakes, spiders. These are fauna, not conversationalists.
  - **Plants** (body type 33) — mushroom men, treants with no script. Non-verbal organisms.
  - **Untargetable/No Target** (body types 11, 28) — environmental entities not meant for interaction.
  - **Golems and basic constructs** — stone golems, lava golems, animated armor. These are magical automata that follow orders; they do not converse. (Named golems with quest scripts are unaffected since scripts take priority.)
  - **Elementals** — summoned or bound magical entities (air, earth, fire, water elementals). They may emit environmental sounds but do not hold conversation.
  - The architect should determine the complete body type exclusion list based on database analysis. The lore-master has confirmed that specific body type IDs for golems and elementals need to be identified.
- **Player has the NPC targeted** and is within Say range (200 units) — standard EQ targeting/distance rules apply.
- **Player is not invisible** to the NPC — standard EQ visibility rules apply.

By the numbers: Of ~61,289 spawned NPCs, approximately 4,894 have INT < 30 and would be excluded. Additional exclusions from body types (animals, plants, untargetable) overlap significantly with the INT filter. After all exclusions and removing scripted NPCs (~7,700), approximately **45,000+ NPCs would gain conversational ability**.

**Lore note on specific creature types** (confirmed by lore-master):
- **Intelligent undead**: Wights, spectres, and liches retain intelligence and SHOULD be able to speak — often menacingly. The INT filter handles this correctly (these creatures have INT >= 30 in the database).
- **Frogloks**: Classic-era Frogloks in Upper/Lower Guk are hostile and feral. They should speak in broken, threatening speech — NOT the friendly, redeemed characterization from the later Legacy of Ykesha expansion. The system prompt must not generate friendly Froglok dialogue.
- **Gnolls/Kobolds**: Wild specimens should be non-verbal or speak in broken growls. City-adjacent gnolls with higher INT may speak broken Common.
- **Ogres/Trolls**: City NPCs in Oggok and Grobb have INT 30–60 and speak — in simple, direct sentences appropriate to their culture. The INT filter correctly preserves this.

#### Faction-to-Behavior Mapping

The NPC's attitude toward the player is determined by the game's existing faction system. There are 9 faction levels in EverQuest. Each maps to a distinct conversational behavior:

| Faction Level | Value | Conversational Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Ally** | 1 | Warm and forthcoming. Shares information freely, offers advice, treats the player as a trusted friend. References shared cause or loyalty. |
| **Warmly** | 2 | Friendly and helpful. Willing to chat, gives useful information, but doesn't share secrets or sensitive details. |
| **Kindly** | 3 | Polite and cooperative. Answers questions helpfully, engages in pleasant conversation. |
| **Amiably** | 4 | Cordial but businesslike. Responds to questions but doesn't volunteer extra information. Keeps things professional. |
| **Indifferently** | 5 | Neutral and reserved. Gives short, factual answers. Not rude, but not warm either. Doesn't go out of their way. |
| **Apprehensively** | 6 | Wary and guarded. Gives terse, cautious answers. May ask the player what their business is. Clearly uncomfortable with the interaction. |
| **Dubiously** | 7 | Suspicious and unfriendly. Minimal responses, may warn the player to leave, openly distrustful. |
| **Threateningly** | 8 | Pre-combat warning. The NPC has not decided to fight but is on the edge. Issues direct warnings, threatens consequences, refuses all help. Tone is city-culture-dependent: a Qeynos guard says "Your kind is not welcome here. Move along before I change my mind." A Freeport guard says "You've got about three seconds to state your business or I'm taking you in." A Neriak guard says nothing — just contempt. Responds to at most one message, then ignores further speech for a cooldown period. |
| **Scowling** | 9 | Openly hostile. The NPC has decided you are an enemy. **No verbal response.** The NPC performs a hostile emote (glares with contempt, snarls, makes a threatening gesture) but refuses all conversation. Further speech attempts are ignored for a cooldown period. This is distinct from Threatening — a Scowling NPC will not waste words on you. |

**Design rationale**: Tying conversation behavior to the existing faction system means that as a player improves their standing with a faction (through quests, kills, or other means), the NPCs of that faction gradually become more talkative and helpful. This creates a tangible, immersive reward for faction work that goes beyond merchant access and aggro changes.

**Threatening/Scowling distinction** (per lore-master review): In EQ lore, Threatening (-700 to -1049 faction) means the NPC is hostile and may attack on sight. Scowling (-1050 to -2000) means the NPC is very hostile and will attack immediately. Threatening NPCs are on the knife's edge — they might still issue a warning. Scowling NPCs have already made their decision. The conversational behavior reflects this: Threatening gives a terse warning, Scowling gives nothing but a hostile emote.

**Hostile cooldown**: At both Threatening (8) and Scowling (9) faction levels, the NPC ignores further speech for 60 seconds after their initial response/emote. This prevents players from spamming hostile NPCs to generate content and waste LLM inference time.

**NPCs outside city zones** (dungeons, wilderness, trade routes) do not receive city cultural context. Their personality comes purely from race, class, and level attributes.

**Luclin knowledge asymmetry** (per lore-master): NPCs on Norrath during the Luclin era know the moon exists and some know of the Nexus spires for travel. But "going to Luclin" is a recent, exotic, and unsettling development for most Norrath residents. A Qeynos guard would NOT casually discuss Luclin as a travel destination — they would treat it as strange, dangerous, and very far away. The system prompt must reflect this: Norrath NPCs may know old legends of "Kerrans" (cat-people exiled from Odus) but would not know the name "Vah Shir" or details of Luclin geography.

#### Typing Indicator Pattern

When the LLM is generating a response, the NPC immediately performs an emote to signal that a response is coming. This bridges the 1–2 second inference gap and makes the delay feel intentional:

- The emote appears in the player's chat window as: *NPC Name considers your words carefully...*
- The emote fires instantly (before the LLM call begins), so the player sees immediate feedback.
- Different emote variants can be used for variety: "ponders your question...", "thinks for a moment...", "studies you briefly..."
- At Scowling faction, the emote is hostile instead: "glares at you with undisguised contempt" — and no verbal response follows.
- The emote is only visible to the player who spoke (not broadcast to the zone).

#### Response Characteristics

NPC responses have these qualities:

- **Length**: 1–3 sentences. Responses are capped at approximately 450 characters to fit comfortably in the EQ chat window without excessive scrolling. Shorter is better — NPCs should sound like they're having a conversation, not delivering a monologue.
- **Voice**: Each NPC speaks in a manner consistent with their race, class, role, and city culture. A gruff dwarf warrior in Kaladim speaks differently from an elegant high elf enchanter in Felwithe. A Freeport militia guard (cynical, world-weary) speaks differently from a Kelethin wood elf ranger (alert, terse, nature-attuned).
- **Deity awareness**: NPCs reference their city's patron deity where natural. A Qeynos guard swears by Rodcet Nife or Karana. A Neriak dark elf invokes Innoruuk. A Freeport guard avoids deity references (Lucan tolerates multiple faiths pragmatically). An Ogre in Oggok references Rallos Zek.
- **Era compliance**: NPCs never reference content, events, locations, or terminology from after the Shadows of Luclin expansion. (See Era Compliance section below for the detailed forbidden and allowed topic lists.)
- **No modern concepts**: NPCs never use modern abstract language. No "mental health" (use "malady of the mind"), no "economy" (use "the trade of goods"), no "democracy" (Qeynos has a Council and a monarch), no "technology" (use "artifice," "craft," or "gnomish engineering"), no "stress" (use "troubled thoughts"). The system prompt must explicitly forbid modern framing and provide in-world equivalents.
- **No meta-knowledge**: NPCs never acknowledge they are in a game, never reference game mechanics by their mechanical names (they say "meditation" not "sitting to regen mana"), and never break character.
- **No quest fabrication**: NPCs do not invent quests, promise rewards, or claim to offer services they cannot actually provide. If an NPC has no quest to give, they talk about lore, local events, their daily life, or direct the player elsewhere. (Phase 1 instruction: "Do not offer quests or promise rewards.")
- **Faction-consistent tone**: Responses match the faction-to-behavior mapping table above. The same question gets a warm answer from an ally and a cold answer from a hostile NPC.

#### Per-NPC Opt-Out

Any specific NPC can be excluded from LLM conversations via a data bucket flag. Setting the flag `llm_enabled` to `0` for a given NPC type ID causes the global hook to skip the LLM call and fall through to default behavior (silence). This allows:

- Disabling LLM for NPCs that produce poor results
- Disabling LLM for NPCs that should remain silent for lore reasons
- Quick emergency shutoff without restarting the server

The default (no flag set) is LLM-enabled for all eligible NPCs. This is an opt-out system, not opt-in, because the goal is maximum coverage with minimum configuration.

### Balance Considerations

#### 1–6 Player Impact

- **Positive**: For a small-group server, conversational NPCs dramatically improve the sense of a living world. Instead of running through silent cities, players can ask guards for directions, chat with merchants, and get flavor dialogue that makes zones feel populated.
- **No gameplay advantage**: NPC conversations provide flavor and atmosphere only. NPCs do not reveal quest spoilers, grant items, provide gameplay tips, or give any mechanical advantage. A player who never talks to an NPC misses nothing except atmosphere.
- **Inference blocking**: The synchronous LLM call blocks the zone process for 1–2 seconds per conversation turn. For 1–6 players, this is acceptable — at most one player is talking to an NPC at any given moment. The typing indicator makes the pause feel natural. If multiple players are in the same zone and one triggers an LLM call, others may notice a brief hitch (they cannot perform actions that require zone process response for ~1–2 seconds). For Phase 1 with 1–6 players, this is an acceptable tradeoff.
- **Resource usage**: The LLM sidecar requires ~4–6 GB of RAM for the quantized model. The server host needs this headroom available. For a 1–6 player server, the existing hardware should have sufficient capacity.

#### Immersion Protection

- **No hallucination of game content**: The system prompt explicitly instructs the LLM not to invent quests, rewards, or game mechanics. This prevents the most damaging form of immersion break — a player being told about a quest that doesn't exist and spending time trying to complete it.
- **Era lock in prompts**: The system prompt constrains the LLM to Classic–Luclin era knowledge. Post-Luclin content is explicitly forbidden with specific examples.
- **Modern concept blocklist**: The system prompt forbids modern abstract language and provides in-world equivalents, preventing the most common form of LLM anachronism.
- **Graceful degradation**: If the LLM produces a response that seems wrong, the player's experience is no worse than it was before — the NPC was previously silent. A slightly off-character response is better than no response at all.
- **Prompt injection defense**: The player's message is clearly separated from the system prompt and instructions. The system prompt instructs the NPC to stay in character and never acknowledge being an AI. While not foolproof, this prevents casual prompt injection.

### Era Compliance

This feature respects the Classic–Luclin era lock through the following constraints:

#### System Prompt Era Boundary

The LLM system prompt explicitly states the world exists in the "Age of Turmoil" and references only Classic, Ruins of Kunark, Scars of Velious, and Shadows of Luclin content. NPCs may occasionally reference "these troubled times" or "the Age of Turmoil" as a natural lore touchstone.

#### Explicitly Forbidden Topics

The system prompt includes specific examples of forbidden content (not just a general era statement):

- **Planes of Power expansion** — the Plane of Knowledge as a travel hub, Quarm, elemental planes as traversable content, planar progression
- **The Berserker class** — does not exist until Gates of Discord
- **"Discord" references** — the plane of Discord, the Discord invasion, Muramites
- **Post-Luclin zones** — Riftseekers Sanctum, MPG Trials, Plane of Valor, Plane of Tactics, etc.
- **Modern real-world concepts** — democracy, technology, mental health, economy as abstract concept, stress/anxiety (use "troubled thoughts" or "seek counsel from your deity")
- **Game mechanics by name** — experience points, hit points, mana pool (use in-world equivalents: "wisdom gained in battle," "wounds," "the weave of magic")
- **Casual Luclin references from Norrath NPCs** — NPCs on Norrath do not casually discuss Luclin. If asked, they express wonder, unease, or ignorance. "I have heard tales of spires that carry travelers to the moon, but such things are beyond my understanding." They do NOT treat it as a routine travel destination.

The system prompt should instruct: "You have no knowledge of the Planes of Power, the Plane of Knowledge as a travel hub, or any events after the opening of the Nexus on Luclin. If asked about such things, express confusion or ignorance in character."

#### Allowed Era Content

NPCs may freely reference:

- **Antonica**: Qeynos, Freeport, Neriak, Erudin, Kaladim, Felwithe, Kelethin, Oggok, Grobb, Halas, Rivervale, Paineel, Ak'Anon, and all Classic-era zones
- **Faydwer**: Kaladim, Felwithe, Kelethin, Ak'Anon, Crushbone, and Faydwer zones
- **Kunark**: Cabilis, Firiona Vie, Field of Bone, Overthere, Sebilis, Veeshan's Peak, and Kunark zones
- **Velious**: Thurgadin, Skyshrine, Kael Drakkel, Temple of Veeshan, and Velious zones
- **Luclin**: Shar Vahl, Shadow Haven, the Nexus, Akheva, and Luclin zones (with the knowledge asymmetry noted above — Norrath NPCs treat Luclin as exotic and distant)
- **Deities**: The full Norrath pantheon, used appropriately per city culture (see Zone Cultural Context table)
- **Major racial histories**: Human kingdoms, elf diaspora, dwarf mining culture, Iksar empire, gnomish invention, Ogre/Troll tribal culture, halfling pastoral life, Erudite intellectual tradition
- **Beastlord class**: Exists in Luclin era (playable by Iksar and Vah Shir) and may be referenced

## Dependencies

- **Local LLM model file**: A Mistral 7B (or equivalent) GGUF model file must be downloaded and placed on the server host. The model is ~4 GB for the Q4_K_M quantization.
- **Docker networking**: The LLM sidecar must be on the same Docker network as the EQEmu server container so the Lua scripts can reach it via HTTP.
- **curl availability**: The `io.popen` + `curl` approach requires `curl` to be installed inside the EQEmu server container. (It likely already is, but the architect should verify.)
- **Sufficient RAM**: The host must have ~4–6 GB of free RAM beyond what the existing akk-stack containers use, for the LLM model.
- **No dependency on Pinecone or any external API** — Phase 1 is entirely local. No cloud services, no API keys, no internet required beyond initial model download.

## Open Questions

1. **Which body types should be excluded?** Body types 11 (Untargetable), 21 (Animal), 28 (No Target), and 33 (Plant) are confirmed candidates. The architect should also identify body type IDs for golems, elementals, and basic constructs — these are lore-mute per lore-master review. (Database counts: 5,317 animals, 2,218 untargetable, 686 plants, 119 no-target.)

2. **What is the actual curl availability inside the EQEmu container?** The `io.popen` + `curl` approach depends on curl being present. The architect should verify this and identify alternatives if needed.

3. **How does `io.popen` interact with the LuaJIT event loop?** The integration plan notes it blocks the zone process. The architect should confirm the exact blocking behavior and whether 1–2 seconds of blocking is safe for zone tick processing.

4. **Should the typing indicator emote be visible to all nearby players or only the speaker?** The NPC-CONVERSATION-SYSTEM doc shows that `Mob::Say()` broadcasts to a 200-unit radius. Emotes may work differently. The architect should determine the best delivery method for a speaker-only emote.

5. **What Mistral 7B variant performs best for this use case?** The integration plan suggests `mistral-7b-instruct-v0.3.Q4_K_M.gguf`. The architect should evaluate whether this is the best model for in-character NPC dialogue, or if another variant (e.g., a fine-tuned RP model) would produce better results.

6. **Global NPC script coexistence**: The existing `global_npc.lua` only has an `event_spawn` handler (for Halloween costumes). Adding `event_say` should work alongside it. The architect should confirm there are no conflicts.

7. **Zone cultural context delivery**: The Zone Cultural Context table defines personality per city. How should this data be structured and delivered to the sidecar? Options include: a static JSON config file in the sidecar, a Lua lookup table in the bridge module, or zone metadata passed with each request. The architect should determine the best approach.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC1: Unscripted NPC responds to speech** — A player targets an unscripted NPC (e.g., a generic guard with no quest file) and types `/say Hello`. The NPC responds with a contextually appropriate in-character message within 3 seconds.

- [ ] **AC2: Scripted NPCs are unaffected** — A player targets an NPC with an existing quest script and types the expected keywords. The NPC responds with its scripted dialogue, exactly as before. The LLM is never invoked.

- [ ] **AC3: Intelligence filter works** — A player targets an animal or mindless creature (INT < 30) and speaks. No LLM response occurs. The NPC remains silent (same as current behavior).

- [ ] **AC4: Faction affects tone** — A player with Ally faction speaks to a guard and receives a warm, helpful response. The same player (or a different character with Scowling faction) speaks to the same guard type and receives a hostile emote with no verbal response.

- [ ] **AC5: Threatening vs Scowling distinction** — A player at Threatening faction receives a terse verbal warning. A player at Scowling faction receives only a hostile emote and no verbal response.

- [ ] **AC6: Typing indicator appears** — When the player speaks to an LLM-enabled NPC, an emote appears immediately (before the LLM response) indicating the NPC is "thinking." The emote is visible to the speaker.

- [ ] **AC7: Sidecar health check passes** — The LLM sidecar's `/v1/health` endpoint returns a successful status indicating the model is loaded and ready.

- [ ] **AC8: Graceful degradation** — If the LLM sidecar is stopped or unreachable, speaking to an unscripted NPC produces no response (no error, no crash, no chat spam). The NPC simply stays silent as before.

- [ ] **AC9: Per-NPC opt-out works** — Setting the `llm_enabled` data bucket to `0` for a specific NPC type ID causes that NPC to stop responding via LLM, even if it would otherwise be eligible.

- [ ] **AC10: Response stays in character and era** — Over 10 test conversations with various NPC types (guard, merchant, guildmaster, priest, commoner) across multiple cities (Qeynos, Freeport, Neriak, Kelethin), all responses are in-character, era-appropriate, reference appropriate patron deities, and do not reference post-Luclin content, real-world concepts, or game mechanics by their mechanical names.

- [ ] **AC11: City culture is reflected** — A guard in Qeynos sounds noticeably different from a guard in Freeport (civic duty vs cynical militia). An Erudin scholar sounds different from an Oggok merchant (formal intellectual vs simple direct speech).

- [ ] **AC12: Response length is appropriate** — All LLM responses fit within the EQ chat window without excessive wrapping. Responses are 1–3 sentences (under ~450 characters).

- [ ] **AC13: Hostile cooldown works** — A player at Threatening or Scowling faction speaks to a hostile NPC, receives a warning/emote, then speaks again within 60 seconds. The second message gets no response.

- [ ] **AC14: Non-sentient creatures stay silent** — Animals, golems, elementals, and plants produce no LLM response when spoken to, regardless of INT stat.

- [ ] **AC15: No modern language** — Over 10 test conversations, no responses contain modern concepts (technology, democracy, mental health, economy) — only in-world equivalents.
