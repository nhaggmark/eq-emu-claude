# Zone NPC Census: Classic Through Luclin

This document provides per-zone NPC population summaries, grouped by faction affiliation. Data is drawn from spawn2 + spawnentry + npc_types joins, limited to Classic-Luclin zones.

## How to Read This Document

Each zone entry shows:
- The major NPC groupings by faction
- Level ranges for each group
- Notable individual NPCs where relevant

"KOS" = generic kill-on-sight hostile with no named faction affiliation.

## Major Classic Zone Profiles

### West Commonlands / East Commonlands

The Commonlands are the main overland travel zones connecting Freeport to the rest of Antonica. They serve as the low-level "proving ground" for Freeport-area characters.

**East Commonlands (170 unique NPCs, levels 1-65):**
- Death Fist Orcs: 24 NPCs, levels 1-20. The primary low-level enemy.
- Freeport Militia (patrolling): 20 NPCs, levels 11-41. Both guards and corrupt agents.
- Commons Residents (neutral travelers): 8 NPCs, levels 30-45.
- Various animals: beetles, wolves, etc.
- Shadowed Men: 3 NPCs, levels 25-26. Mysterious entities, lore-relevant.

**West Commonlands (115 unique NPCs, levels 1-99):**
- Death Fist Orcs: 12 NPCs, levels 1-15.
- Dervish Cutthroats: 11 NPCs, levels 10-14 (both human and half-elf variants).
- Shadowed Men: 3 NPCs, levels 25-26.
- Werewolves: 5 NPCs, levels 20-32.
- Will-O-Wisps: 3 NPCs.

### The Greater Faydark

The Greater Faydark is the sprawling elven forest of Faydwer, home to the treehouse city Kelethin and the High Elf city of Felwithe. It is one of the richest zones in the game for NPC variety.

**251 unique NPCs, levels 1-70, 27 racial types, 28 faction groups.**

Major populations:
- Emerald Warriors: 55 NPCs (levels 38-61) — High Elf forest defenders of Tunare
- Crushbone Orcs: 21 NPCs (levels 1-12) — constantly threatening the forest
- Fairies: 10 NPCs (levels 6-21)
- Faydarks Champions: 10 NPCs — Wood Elf rangers
- Arboreans (tree spirits): 5 NPCs
- Clerics of Tunare: 3 NPCs — religious guardians
- Karana Bandits: 6 NPCs — human outlaws

The Emerald Warriors are the dominant defensive force. They are high level (38-61) relative to the zone's starting content, serving as elite protectors.

### Kithicor Forest

One of the most lore-rich zones in Classic EQ. Kithicor appears normal during daylight (low-level forest content) but becomes infested with high-level undead at night due to an ancient battle between the dead wizard Bloody Kithicor and his former allies.

**190 unique NPCs, levels 1-99, 29 race types, 32 faction groups.**

Day population (levels 1-20): woodland animals, scouts, rangers.
Night population: levels 25-99 undead rise throughout the zone, killing unprepared travelers.

### The Rathe Mountains

The most faction-diverse zone in Classic EQ with 49 distinct faction groups and 328 unique NPCs.

**328 unique NPCs, levels 1-99, 38 race types.**

This zone serves as a crossroads between multiple racial territories:
- Hill Giants (levels 35-45) — dominant large creature faction
- Aviaks (bird-people) — multiple factions
- Ogres — Rallos Zek worshippers
- Various wandering creature types

### Castle Mistmoore

Classic vampire dungeon in Faydwer.

**153 unique NPCs, levels 15-45, 10 race types.**

- Primary faction: Mayong Mistmoore (148 NPCs in server) — the vampire lord's servants
- Undead: various vampire, skeleton, and zombie types
- Dark Elves serving Mayong
- Gargoyles

Mayong Mistmoore is one of EQ's iconic villains — an ancient vampire who predates even Luclin-era events.

### Nagafen's Lair (SolB) / Solusek's Eye (SolA)

Twin dungeon zones in Lavastorm Mountains. Nagafen's Lair is home to Lord Nagafen, a red dragon whose faction dominates the zone.

**SolB: 69 NPCs, levels 17-99, 12 race types.**

- Goblins of Fire Peak: Core occupant
- Lord Nagafen's faction: ~5 key dragon NPCs
- Kobolds: assorted cave dwellers

**SolA (Solusek's Eye): 139 NPCs, levels 18-99.**

- Goblins of Fire Peak: 19 NPCs (levels 9-24) — primary occupant
- Various elemental and fire creatures

### The Plane of Fear

Classic raid plane for level 46+ characters.

**97 NPCs, levels 20-70, 21 race types.**

- Inhabitants of Fear — Cazic-Thule's servants
- Amygdalans (4-armed creatures)
- Tentacle Terrors
- Dracoliche (dragon undead)
- Cazic-Thule himself (deity encounter)

### The Ruins of Old Guk / City of Guk

The Guk dungeons are the primary mid-level dungeon complex of Classic EQ, inhabited by Frogloks.

**Old Guk: 170 NPCs, levels 2-60, 13 race types.**
**City of Guk: 143 NPCs, levels 3-99.**

- Frogloks of Guk (251 total server-wide) — the main inhabitants
- Undead Frogloks of Guk (317 server-wide) — the lower levels have undead froglok variants
- Both factions coexist in tension within the dungeon

## Major Kunark Zone Profiles

### Firiona Vie

The main good-aligned outpost in Kunark, surrounded by hostile territory.

**267 unique NPCs, levels 22-99, 25 race types, 18 faction groups.**

- Inhabitants of Firiona Vie (248): 118 NPCs — the village's population
- Various hostile Kunark factions threatening the outpost

### Karnor's Castle

Classic Kunark mid-tier dungeon.

**127 NPCs, levels 39-70, 10 race types.**

- Drusella Sathir's Faction — undead Iksar controlled by Venril Sathir's former wife
- Sarnak (dragon-Iksar hybrid): powerful undead faction

### Chardok

Large Sarnak dungeon.

**221 NPCs, levels 1-71, 12 race types.**

- Brood of Di'Zok (451 total): 183 NPCs — ruling Sarnak faction
- Various Sarnak warrior/caster types

### The Field of Bone / Lake of Ill Omen

Entry zones for Kunark, adjacent to Cabilis.

**Field of Bone: 150 NPCs, levels 1-99, 15 race types.**
**Lake of Ill Omen: 193 NPCs, levels 1-65, 17 race types.**

- Sarnak Collective (307): dominant hostile faction
- Frogloks of Kunark (252): 132 total NPCs — different species from Guk frogloks
- Burynai Legion (253): 98 total — underground insect-like creatures
- Legion of Cabilis patrol NPCs near the city

## Major Velious Zone Profiles

### Kael Drakkel

Home of the Storm Giants (Kromzek and Kromrif factions). One of the most NPC-dense zones.

**512 unique NPCs, levels 1-99, 8 race types, 21 faction groups.**

- Kromzek (448): 258 NPCs — Storm Giants of Kael
- Kromrif (419): 312 total — Ice Giants (allied with Kael)
- These factions are hostile to Coldain (Thurgadin dwarves) and the Claws of Veeshan (dragons)

### Skyshrine

Dragon city (Claws of Veeshan faction).

**617 unique NPCs, levels 1-80, 20 race types, 12 faction groups.**

- Claws of Veeshan (430): dominant faction — dragons and their servants

### The City of Thurgadin

Coldain Dwarf city.

**232 NPCs, levels 1-99, 10 race types, 6 faction groups.**

- Coldain (406): dominant faction — 361 NPCs total in Velious

The Coldain have a complex relationship with the Claws of Veeshan (allied vs Kael) while being enemies of the Kromzek and Kromrif.

## Major Luclin Zone Profiles

### Sanctus Seru

**689 NPCs — largest NPC population of any single zone.**

- Citizens of Seru (1499): 251 NPCs
- Validus Custodus (1503): 229 NPCs — military/guard
- The city represents the conservative Seru Loyalist faction, hostile to Katta Castellum

### Grimling Forest

**639 NPCs, 11 race types.**

- Grimlings of the Forest (1516): 380 NPCs — the dominant Luclin native race

Grimlings are savage, tribal creatures native to Luclin's surface. They occupy most outdoor zones on Luclin in large numbers.

### The City of Shar Vahl

Home of the Vah Shir (cat-people).

**339 NPCs, levels 1-70, 22 race types, 9 faction groups.**

- Citizens of Shar Vahl (1584): 145 NPCs
- Guardians of Shar Vahl (1513): 113 NPCs

The Vah Shir are native to Luclin, having been transported there by Odus-related magical events in the ancient past. Their city is a hub for Beastlord and Warrior characters.

### Katta Castellum

**448 NPCs, levels 1-99, 19 race types, 19 faction groups.**

Described above in faction profiles. The most faction-complex of the Luclin cities, hosting refugees from multiple Norrath races.

## Racial Distribution in Major Cities

Data from city zone queries showing what races appear as NPCs:

### Qeynos (South + North)
Primarily Human population with some Half Elves, Gnomes, and Halflings. Guards are mostly Human (race 1). Merchants vary by guild.

### Freeport (West + East + North)
Very racially diverse — the most cosmopolitan of the Classic cities. Humans dominate but Barbarians, Half Elves, and others appear. The evil underbelly includes Shadow Knight NPCs.

### Neriak (Foreign Quarter + Commons + 3rd Gate)
Dark Elves (race 6) dominate. Foreign Quarter has more mixed races (Trolls working as guards, occasional others). 3rd Gate is almost exclusively Dark Elves.

### Cabilis East + West
Entirely Iksar (race 128). The only Iksar-exclusive city. A few Froglok servants appear.

### Katta Castellum
Mixed races: High Elves, Humans, Half Elves, and other "civilized" races from the old Combine Empire. No Trolls, Ogres, or Iksar.

### Shar Vahl
Primarily Vah Shir (race 130) — a cat-like humanoid race exclusive to Luclin. The rare mixed-race inhabitants are traders.
